Lifting Lug Evaluation

Method: Rajendra – Design/Evaluation of Overhead Lifting Lugs (PDHonline S106) · Dunwoody Engineering

Evaluates an overhead lifting lug for seven failure modes: tensile rupture across the pin hole, bearing on the pin, shear tear-out, tensile block failure beyond the pin hole, out-of-plane buckling (thickness check), weld shear/tension, and base material tension at the lug root.

Applied load W may be angled in-plane (α) and out-of-plane (β). The calculator reports the allowable load for each failure mode and the governing (minimum) capacity.

Failure Modes & Method Summary

This calculator follows the method in Rajendra – Design/Evaluation of Overhead Lifting Lugs (PDHonline Course S106). Seven failure modes are evaluated:

  1. Tensile rupture across pin hole — net section at hole: Pw1 = 0.6·Fy·t·(w−d)
  2. Pin bearing — bearing on lug plate: Pw2 = 0.9·Fy·t·dpin, where dpin = d − 0.5 in
  3. Shear tear-out — double shear plane beyond hole: Pw3 = 0.4·Fu·t·2·a_eff
  4. Tensile block beyond hole — 0.8d block: Pw4 = 0.6·Fu·t·0.8·d
  5. Out-of-plane buckling — thickness check t ≥ 0.5 in (no load reduction)
  6. Weld — combined tension + bending on weld group (fillet): Pw6
  7. Base metal at lug root — tensile stress from axial + bending: Pw7

Geometry Rule 1: a ≥ d/2  |  Rule 2: d ≤ 1.25·a (otherwise a is reduced to a_eff = d/1.25)
